Decentralised solar-biogas systems for resilient communities

BioSolar Nexus is developing a modular off-grid platform that combines photovoltaic-thermal generation, anaerobic digestion, biogas-fuelled CHP, thermal storage, battery support and IoT-enabled control for crisis-affected and energy-insecure settings.

Why it matters

Built for resilience, practicality and scale-up

The project responds to the need for decentralised, climate-resilient infrastructure that can operate in difficult conditions and support multiple community needs through one integrated system.

Energy resilience

Supports local electricity and heat generation where grid access is unstable, damaged or limited.

Waste to value

Converts organic waste streams into useful biogas and digestate-based outputs.

Cold-climate readiness

Designed around thermal buffering and system integration for year-round operation, including harsh weather conditions.

Community benefit

Connects energy access, sanitation, food-related use cases and local resilience in one modular concept.

System concept

How BioSolar Nexus works

The platform integrates proven technologies into a single modular system adapted for off-grid and crisis-affected deployment.

01

PVT generation

Photovoltaic-thermal modules provide electrical output together with usable thermal energy.

02

Anaerobic digestion

Organic waste is processed to generate biogas for decentralised energy production.

03

Biogas CHP

A micro-CHP unit converts biogas into both electricity and heat for practical on-site use.

04

PCM thermal storage

Phase change materials help stabilise thermal conditions and improve cold-climate operation.

05

Battery support

Modular battery storage improves resilience and continuity of supply.

06

IoT monitoring

Sensors, control logic and remote diagnostics support operation, data collection and optimisation.

Project goals

What the project is aiming to deliver

  • Develop and validate an integrated modular solar-biogas system
  • Demonstrate the concept through deployment activity in Ukraine
  • Generate technical, operational and social learning for future replication
  • Support dissemination, exploitation and long-term scale-up pathways
